<p><p>Bio-pesticides are classified into three main types: Bioinsecticides, which target insects, Biofungicides, which combat fungal diseases, and Bioherbicides, which control weeds. Bioinsecticides are derived from natural sources and disrupt insect growth or development. Biofungicides contain beneficial microorganisms that suppress fungal growth. Bioherbicides use natural compounds to inhibit weed growth. These bio-pesticides are environmentally friendly alternatives to chemical pesticides, as they have minimal impact on non-target organisms and ecosystems. They are gaining popularity in agricultural practices due to their effectiveness and sustainability.</p></p>
