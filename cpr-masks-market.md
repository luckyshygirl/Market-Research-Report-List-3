<p><p>CPR masks, also known as pocket masks or barrier devices, are masks designed to be used during cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) procedures to provide a barrier between the rescuer's mouth and the patient's respiratory tract. These masks help to prevent cross-contamination and protect both the rescuer and the patient from the transmission of infectious diseases.</p><p>The CPR masks market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing awareness about the importance of immediate response during a cardiac emergency has led to a higher demand for CPR training programs, which in turn drives the adoption of CPR masks. Additionally, the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and the growing geriatric population contribute to the demand for CPR masks.</p><p>Moreover, the introduction of technologically advanced CPR masks with features like one-way valves, filters, and oxygen inlet ports has further boosted the market growth. These advanced masks enhance the effectiveness of CPR procedures and offer better protection against contaminants.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has augmented the demand for CPR masks, as healthcare professionals and individuals have become more cautious about infection control. <p><p>The CPR masks market comprises various types of masks used during cardiopulmonary resuscitation procedures. Facial masks are positioned over the patient's mouth and nose for oxygenation, while laryngeal masks are placed into the patient's throat to secure their airway. Oral masks cover only the patient's mouth, while nasal pillow masks fit into the patient's nostrils. Each type of mask has its own advantages and specific applications in different CPR scenarios. Understanding the characteristics and functionalities of these masks helps medical professionals make informed decisions for providing efficient and appropriate resuscitation care.</p></p>
