<p><p>Surface-active substances, or surfactants, are classified into different market types based on their chemical properties and actions. These include anionic surfactants, which have a negative charge and are commonly used in cleaning products; non-ionic surfactants, which do not have a charge and are gentle on the skin; cationic surfactants, which have a positive charge and are often used as germicides; amphoteric surfactants, which can act as both anionic and cationic surfactants depending on pH; and other specialized surfactants used for specific applications.</p></p>
