<p><p>The porcine vaccine market includes several types of vaccines that are used to protect pigs from various diseases. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, while live attenuated vaccines contain weakened pathogens. Toxoid vaccines are made from the toxins produced by pathogens. Recombinant vaccines use genetically engineered pathogens or proteins. Conjugate vaccines combine specific pathogen antigens with a carrier molecule. DNA vaccines use genetic material from pathogens. These different types of vaccines offer a range of options for preventing diseases in pigs.</p></p>
