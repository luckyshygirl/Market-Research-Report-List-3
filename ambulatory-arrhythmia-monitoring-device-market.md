<p><p>Ambulatory arrhythmia monitoring devices are used to track heart rhythms outside of traditional clinical settings. There are two main types of devices: external and implantable. External devices are worn on the body and are typically used for short-term monitoring. Implantable devices are surgically inserted under the skin and can provide continuous monitoring for longer periods of time. Both types of devices help healthcare providers diagnose and manage arrhythmias in patients, allowing for personalized treatment plans and improved patient outcomes.</p></p>
