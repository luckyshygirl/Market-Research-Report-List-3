<p><p>Refrigeration inorganic phase change materials can be classified into two main types: Type I and Type II. Type I materials have a high latent heat capacity and can efficiently absorb and release large amounts of thermal energy during phase change, making them suitable for applications requiring high cooling capacity. On the other hand, Type II materials have a lower latent heat capacity but offer faster heat transfer rates, making them better suited for applications where rapid temperature stabilization is crucial. Each type caters to specific needs within the refrigeration industry.</p></p>
