<p><p>Optical Subassemblies (OSA) are key components in optical communication systems. There are four main types: TOSA (Transmitter Optical Subassembly), BOSA (Bidirectional Optical Subassembly), ROSA (Receiver Optical Subassembly), and POSA (Passive Optical Subassembly). TOSA includes a laser diode for transmitting signals, while ROSA consists of a photodetector for receiving signals. BOSA combines both functions in one package. POSA, on the other hand, serves as a passive component without any active optical elements. Each type plays a crucial role in the transmission and reception of optical signals.</p></p>
