<p><p>Refractory products are divided into two main market types: clay and non-clay. Clay refractories are made from naturally occurring minerals, such as kaolin or fireclay, and are known for their high resistance to heat and abrasion. Non-clay refractories, on the other hand, are made from a variety of materials, including silica, magnesia, and alumina. They offer superior performance in extreme temperatures and harsh environments. Both market types cater to industries requiring heat-resistant materials, like steel, cement, and glass manufacturing.</p></p>
