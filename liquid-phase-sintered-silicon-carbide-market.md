<p><ul><li>Reaction Sintered</li><li>Atmospheric Pressure Sintered</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Liquid Phase Sintered Silicon Carbide is categorized into two main types: Reaction Sintered and Atmospheric Pressure Sintered. Reaction Sintering involves the reaction between silicon and carbon to form silicon carbide at high temperatures. This method results in dense and high-quality silicon carbide products. On the other hand, Atmospheric Pressure Sintering involves directly sintering silicon carbide powders under atmospheric pressure. This method is cost-effective and suitable for producing large quantities of silicon carbide with good mechanical properties.</p></p>
