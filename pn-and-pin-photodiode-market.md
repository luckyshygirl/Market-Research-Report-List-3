PN photodiodes have a simple structure with a P-N junction, while PIN photodiodes have an additional intrinsic layer between the P and N regions. PIN photodiodes offer higher sensitivity and lower capacitance compared to PN photodiodes, making them suitable for high-speed and low-light applications. Both types are widely used in various industries such as telecommunications, medical devices, and automotive sensors.</p></p>
