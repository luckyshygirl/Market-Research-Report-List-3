<p><p>Live cell imaging microscopes are essential tools in biological research for observing and analyzing living cells in real time. Two common types of live cell imaging microscopes are confocal laser scanning microscopes and spinning-disk confocal microscopes. Confocal laser scanning microscopes use a laser to create high-resolution images by eliminating out-of-focus light, allowing for 3D imaging of thick specimens. Spinning-disk confocal microscopes use a spinning disk with multiple pinholes to rapidly capture images, enabling high-speed imaging of dynamic cellular processes. Both types are widely used in cell biology research.</p></p>
