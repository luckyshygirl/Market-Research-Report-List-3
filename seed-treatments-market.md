<p><p>Seed treatments refer to methods used to protect seeds from diseases and pests, promoting healthy plant growth. Chemical seed treatment involves using synthetic pesticides and fungicides to control pathogens. Non-chemical seed treatments, on the other hand, utilize biological agents, such as beneficial microorganisms and plant extracts, to achieve the same goal without using harmful chemicals. Both types of seed treatments aim to improve seed quality, increase crop yield, and reduce the reliance on conventional pesticides for agriculture.</p></p>
